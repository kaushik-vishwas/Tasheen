Sourcing Sustainable Fabrics

Not all fabrics are created equally! We source fabrics that are sustainably produced, working with suppliers that share our ethical values so that our customers can shop for high quality with a conscience.

Lotus

The lotus, India's national flower, signifies purity and rebirth. Crafting one meter of fabric requires 32,000 lotus stems, extracted by skilled artisans. This process, from bloom to loom, captures centuries of tradition and innovation. A single scarf takes two months of meticulous work. Picture the magic as a petal transforms into a thread, weaving elegance and comfort. Lotus fabric transcends, offering a sensory journey celebrating nature's beauty and transformation. Appreciate the lotus's enchanting journey from pond depths to luxury pinnacle.

Lotus

Milk

Every year, billions of liters of milk are wasted or spoiled worldwide. However, surprisingly, these very sources of raw materials have become the foundation for the milk fabric fashion industry – a promising and environmentally friendly material of the future. In contrast to consuming milk, Tasheen is getting you to “wear” milk through the use of milk fabric. A wonderfully creative product, we’re transforming week old soured milk into silky-smooth high end fashion fabric.

Milk

Rose

The Rose Petal Fiber has been extracted from the natural waste of rose petals. The fiber has been stripped and processed to create a luxurious and soft spinning fiber, similar to silk. In addition to being cultivated without the use of any chemicals and a being a biodegradable fiber, the antioxidants contained in rose petals fight off free radicals, skin irritants, excess sebum and germs, cooling and smoothing sensitive parts of the body and supporting the nervous system

Rose

Banana

Years of research and development resulted into a new material that answers the question of economic, environmental and social sustainability, and that is the fabric made from the banana fruit. This fiber is made from the peels of the banana fruit which would otherwise go through atrocious wastage and full up the landfills all over the world this causing pollution. Tasheen is committed towards towards zero fruit loss and reduced landfills necessary for carbon neutrality.

Banana

Orange

The fashion industry is getting a vitamin boost from a new innovation called Orange Fiber. This sustainable fabric is made from discarded citrus fruit peels, reducing landfill waste. It's biodegradable and can even be composted. What's unique is that it's enriched with vitamins (A, C, and E) and natural oils, making it like a wearable body cream that nourishes the wearer's skin without feeling greasy.

Orange

Bamboo

One of the most sustainable crops on the planet, Tasheen is using bamboo fiber to make clothing. Because the root isn’t removed during harvesting, this improves soil health and supports biodiversity. TASHEEN believes in the difference bamboo clothing makes in the world. Bamboo absorbs five times more carbon than hardwood trees. It needs half the land cotton needs to produce the same amount of fibre and it doesn’t need irrigation or pesticides. Add to that how well it works as a performance fabric and you can see why we love bamboo.
